What is ChalkPix?

The Problem and the Solution

Online classes are always recorded and streamed in traditional video format. While that is fine in many cases, this format makes it hard for people with low bandwidth to have a bufferless experience and also in the current scenario of meetings and live classes with hundreds of people in a single call, it gets very network heavy and lag between video and audio becomes an issue.

One optimisation we can do in this area is to make specialised formats for specific methodologies of teachings. Even though there have been many advancements in teaching methodologies, the method of writing on board and teaching never gets old. Whether it be deep learning lectures by Andrew Ng or additions lecture by convent school teacher, most people prefer using pen tool to teach even in online mode. There is no point in trying to screencast every pixel of the recorded screen to all the viewers in this kind of teaching.

Although efficient video coding algorithms exist to compress the video, we believe it would more efficient to just stream mouse movements and events of the whiteboard instead of screenshare. While we haven't done any benchmark tests, we believe this will lessen the data to be stored for whiteboard lectures dramatically and help give the best experience across all devices irrespective of the quality of stream.



Existing Solutions

Whiteboard solutions aren't a new thing. Zoom supports it but the recording is still a video. Microsoft Teams supports it but has no option of recording the session yet. Although these solutions exist, they work differently from how we are thinking of providing the service.

The only product that seems to be close to what we are trying to build is an Indian ed-tech startup 'Vedantu'. Although they haven't revealed the internal workings of the app, we believe they are following a similar approach based on the vague terms from their tech blogs.

Even with these solutions, we believe having a open source solution that can be plugged with any kind of software in any kind of purpose wouldn't harm.



Roadmap

  • Create a data format(.cpx) that efficiently stores the mouse movements and events. The format must be usable for both streaming and offline viewing purposes.
  • Create a full-fledged whiteboard with capability to store a recording in .cpx format or stream to a certain registered URL.
  • Create a web component chalk-pix that works similar to other HTML media elements like video and audio. The chalk-pix can take a source link to .cpx file that is present on a server or a stream URL of a live recording. This is similar to media elements.
  • Dicey decision(s):
    • Integrate image, gif, video in .cpx format


Concepts borrowed from


  • InkML: This is an open standard for ink based streaming and recording that was last updated in 2011. Most of the concepts required to build chalk-pix are already covered in this specification. But there is no industry standard implementation that exists or to refer to and also many browsers aren't supporting it natively. Even with them, the specification doesn't deal with modern needs like polygon shapes, in-board images, slides etc.
  • MPEG DASH: This shouldn't come as a surprise since chalk-pix deals with streaming and there is no better streaming standard currently than DASH(Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP). While there isn't much adpative streaming needed when only pen and shapes are used, there may be a requirement when in-board images and videos are used. Also, it is required to adapt audio quality if the bandwidth is too poor.


How to test the current progress

  • Go to 'Recorder' by clicking on the button above. Give microphone permission.
  • Click 'Start recording/streaming' and try to draw something on the canvas using the tools given.
  • To watch how streaming is working, click on 'Go to player' button which opens 'ChalkPix Player' in new tab. Select 'Stream' radio button. Enter the meeting id of recording in given text field and click submit. Give microphone permission. Once permission is given, you should be able to watch the recording live.
  • If you want to store the recording and view it, press 'Stop Recording/Streaming' button in recording tab. The 'Download .cpx' button should be enabled. Click on it to download .cpx file. Then, go to player as mentioned in previous point and click on 'Archive' radio button. Upload the file using 'browse' button and press play.
